About Me

I study Information Technology at DTU with a focus on cybersecurity, and most of my serious work sits at the seam between those two worlds — specifically, how you stop an autonomous agent from doing something it wasn't supposed to do.

That question led to a 1st-place finish at ArmorIQ, where I built intent-enforcement architecture that constrained LLM execution boundaries inside financial systems. It also shapes the research I care about most: retrieval-augmented generation, knowledge graphs, and self-correcting pipelines — the plumbing that makes AI systems reliable rather than just impressive.

On the engineering side, I build full-stack: FastAPI backends, React frontends, vector databases, the works. I prefer shipping things that hold up under pressure — hackathons have a way of testing that.
